Photo Name of Dish Description Origin
Adobo - A slow-cooked dish that usually has marinated meat as the prime ingredient. This dish is the Philippines' national dish. - name originated from the word "adobar" which means "to marinate"
Sinigang - Another meat dish with a delightfully sour soup. Its main ingredient is a tamarind-flavored broth, and vegetables such as tomatoes, eggplant, and onions. This dish is one of the Philippines' famous dishes. - it is of unknown origin as of now, but can be traced back before the Philippines was colonized.
Tapa - Usually made with beef, this dish is always paired with rice and a fried egg. It has many variations which always end with "silog". - origin is not really known, though they are derived from Proto-Austronesian "Capa".
Bulalo - made by simmering beef bone marrow. - name originated from the word "bula" wich means bone marrow.
Lomi - Made with primarily eggs, pork liver, kikiam, all resting in a thick broth then topped with more eggs. - in 1968, this dish was created by To Kim Eng in Lipa, Batangas.
